\section{Groups}

\begin{quest}
What is a normal subgroup? Can you get some natural map from a normal subgroup? What topological objects can the original group, normal subgroup, and quotient group relate to?
\end{quest}

\begin{proof}[Answer]
A subgroup $H\subseteq G$ is \textit{normal} if all conjugation of $H$ is itself, i.e. $\forall g\in G$, $H=g^{-1}Hg$. The normal subgroup defines the quotient group. The natural map is $H\hookrightarrow G \rightarrow G/H$. ``Sub'' means embedding, and ``quotient'' means gluing.
\end{proof}
